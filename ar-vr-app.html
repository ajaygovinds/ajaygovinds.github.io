Introduction

As we delve into this case study, I'm thrilled to walk you through the captivating journey of the AR Proof of Concept (POC) project and the design intricacies of the VR app interface. While you may be familiar with the project from the Web app case study, it's essential to note that concurrently, our team developed a VR version with the precise intention of aligning its launch date with the web app. Join me as we navigate the creative process, delving into the distinctive challenges and innovative solutions that guided me through both of these projects.



Objective

In steering the AR POC app project, my primary goal was to assess the feasibility of in-house development within our constraints of limited resources.

On the VR front, the objective shifted towards exploring the immersive dimension. The aim was to envision and understand how the web app experience could seamlessly translate into the VR headset environment.

Research & Discovery: Embracing the Realms of AR and VR Development

Diving into the AR POC project demanded a deep dive into the intricacies of AR development. My journey involved mastering the development pipeline, acquainting myself with various AR-compatible devices, and understanding the nuanced landscape of AR technology.

Transitioning to VR, the focus shifted to building familiarity with a spectrum of head-mounted devices. This presented a unique opportunity to explore a diverse array of VR headsets and associated devices, all while comprehending the intricate development pipeline. I delved into understanding the essential components, considering ergonomics for development, and ensuring code maintenance aligned with VR standards.

Throughout both realms, I meticulously observed interfaces, contemplating the optimal color schemes for AR applications and VR experiences. This immersive research phase laid the foundation for informed decision-making as we ventured further into the AR and VR design territories.



Ideation & Design Process: Crafting AR Simplicity and VR Precision

Navigating the ideation and design process for the AR POC app initially seemed straightforward - a blend of brainstorming and development. However, as clarity unfolded, it became evident that decisions on gestures and optimization were critical. The iterative process involved brainstorming, creating rough sketches, defining gestures, crafting 3D components, diving into code, and rigorous testing. Given the exploratory nature of a Proof of Concept, formal mockups were deemed unnecessary.

Contrastingly, the design journey for the VR app proved more exhaustive. Catering to a medical context demanded precision in gesture accuracy. To achieve this, collaboration with Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) became paramount. The detailed requirements were meticulously gathered through a process involving storyboarding, blocking, creating UI interfaces, prototyping, debugging, and thorough testing. This rigorous methodology ensured that the VR app met the stringent standards required for medical applications.



Conclusion

Having presented the AR POC for review, valuable feedback was received, and it remains a work in progress as I pen down this case study. The iterative nature of this process ensures continuous refinement and enhancement.

In the realm of VR, the culmination of efforts resulted in a successful launch, along with the web app release highlighted in the "Web app" case study. This significant milestone unfolded during the C20 IHH event at Amrita Hospital, Faridabad. Witnessing the VR app in action was not just a testament to our dedication but also a realization of the immersive possibilities that virtual reality bring to the forefront of medical applications.
