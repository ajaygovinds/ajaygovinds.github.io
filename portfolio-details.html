Introduction

In the realm of education, accessibility to practical learning tools remains paramount. Many schools face challenges in providing hands-on science experiences due to budget constraints or lack of resources.

Recognizing this gap, an educational initiative pioneered by Amrita CREATE[1], the Amrita Center for Research in Analytics, Technologies & Education at Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham[2] in partnership with CDAC, Mumbai; under a research grant from MeitY, Government of India[3] developed OLabs[4], enabling students to learn experiments, explore concepts, and foster a love for science and other subjects from anywhere.



Objective

Introduce a 3D simulation for enhanced learning, ensuring seamless integration and boosting engagement for school students.

I used to work as a Graphic Designer before moving to the Project Engineer role. Our organization had a vision to integrate 3D simulations into the platform. Even though I had just the basics of the software, I tried my best to learn and contribute to this larger goal.



Research & Discovery

  • Worked with a physics expert to understand the basics of pendulum movement for the simulation.
  • Used simple math to make the pendulum's motion look right.
  • Learned about the software tools needed, with some advice from the expert.
  • Together with the expert, made sure the simulation showed pendulum physics clearly.


Ideation & Design Process

Growing up, I've always enjoyed playing computer games. Drawing inspiration from those experiences, I aimed to make the 3D simulation more engaging by introducing simple keyboard shortcuts for movements and actions.

While wireframing wasn't as essential due to the 3D nature of the project, I recognized the need for prototyping. Additionally, I spent time understanding the formulas to ensure everything worked accurately.



Challenges

The first challenge was getting the 3D model ready for our development process. We had to make many adjustments to fit it in.

After that, when we finished the simulation, combining it with our platform had its own set of problems. There were many things to consider and some parts didn't fit easily.



Conclusion

After finishing the first test phase, we added it to the platform. Feeling confident from this test, we started building more 3D simulations for school kids to learn for free.
